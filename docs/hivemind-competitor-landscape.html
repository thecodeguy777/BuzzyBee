How the market actually works

Buyers pay for two halves — and stitch them together themselves.

Half 1 — the person

What every competitor sells: a vetted, managed assistant. ~$1,000–$3,000/mo offshore; $2,500–$6,500/mo for premium US talent.

Crowded · commodity

Half 2 — the software (on you)

The VA logs into your tools. You still buy CRM, dialer, chat, project mgmt, meetings, time-tracking, scheduling — per seat — and wire them together.

The "software tax"

HiveMind collapses both halves into one product. That's the move nobody else makes.

Who you're up against

$2.6k–$6.5k/mo · no tools

Offshore managed VAs

$700–$3k/mo · portal at most

placement fee · zero tools

Across all three, the work software is the customer's problem. The differentiation isn't "we have VAs" — it's what the VA works inside.

How the market charges

Four pricing models

1 · Hourly / hour-bundles

Pre-buy hours (often rollover). Time etc ~$30–38/hr, Double ~$55/hr, Magic $10–35/hr.

2 · Dedicated FTE, flat monthly

The dominant offshore model. Wing $999, MyOutDesk $1,988, Athena $3,000, Wishup $2,849 — full-time.

3 · Premium US, quote-only

Talk-to-sales, opaque. Belay ~$42–46/hr; Boldly $2,600+/mo. Quality & compliance as the price story.

4 · Placement / DIY

Somewhere ~35% of annual salary one-time; OnlineJobs.ph $69–99/mo to self-recruit, then pay the VA direct.

Rough center of gravity: ~$1,500–$2,500/mo basic offshore full-time, ~$2,500–$4,000/mo specialized — before any software.

The hidden bill

The "software tax" is real & documented

Industry VA guides say a VA needs "3–4 tools that work well together." In practice it's 6–8 per-seat subscriptions the buyer pays on top of the person:

Comms + meetings

Slack / Teams + Zoom

Project mgmt

Asana / Trello / ClickUp / Monday

CRM + support

HubSpot / Freshdesk (~$15/agent/mo)

Dialer / calling

Aircall / PhoneBurner / Mojo

Time + scheduling

Toggl/Clockify + Calendly (~$8–12/seat)

Automation + design

Zapier (~$20+) + Canva (~$15)

MyOutDesk says it plainly: its VAs "work within YOUR dialer and CRM." The customer owns, pays, and stitches all of it — that's the tax HiveMind removes.

The wedge

Where HiveMind wins

  • ✓Depth of the included suite. Not a task portal — the actual work tools (chat, CRM, tasks, dialer, meetings, time) as one connected hive. This is the part nobody bundles.
  • ✓"People, not per-seat SaaS." 100% of spend buys work; tools cost $0 on top. The competitor's customer is still paying a separate software bill.
  • ✓General-purpose, many verticals. Coaches, agencies, e-commerce, wellness, hospitality, pro services, real estate, construction — generalists have no "sacred" tool to defend, so "tools included" is an easy yes.
  • ✓One bill, one login, no stitching. You also kill the integration/setup burden — the silent cost of running 6–8 disconnected apps.
Eyes open

The honest risks

  • !"Tools included" isn't unheard-of. Wing leans on its workspace app. Win on depth (real revenue tools), not the phrase.
  • !Tool-loyal verticals resist. Real estate (Follow Up Boss/kvCORE) and construction (Buildertrend/Procore) won't drop their stack. Don't lead with them.
  • !You're a staffing co and a 6-app SaaS co. Keeping all six modern is real, ongoing cost — that's the burden behind the moat.
  • ×"More modern UI" is copyable. Looks are the easiest thing to clone. The defensible part is integration + switching cost once a team runs inside the hive.
The play

Horizontal product. Vertical go-to-market.

Build for everyone (you do)

The hive is vertical-agnostic. Keep the identity constant: people, not software tax — one connected hive.

Market to the easy-yes few first

Lead ads with coaches, agencies, e-commerce, wellness, hospitality — no sacred tools, heavy admin. Verticals = proof, not identity.

  • →Compete on suite depth + transparent pricing vs. the quote-only incumbents.
  • →Frame the rival as "a VA + a pile of subscriptions," not any single company.
  • →Save real estate & construction for later — they're the hardest "drop your tools" sell.
