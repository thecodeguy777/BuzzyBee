01Executive Summary

The Philippine IT-BPM sector generated roughly $40 billion in export revenue in 2025, with projections near $42 billion in 2026 and a $59 billion target by 2028. Within it, the virtual-assistant segment is one of the fastest-growing: the global VA market sits around $19.6 billion in 2025 with a compound annual growth rate above 20%.

We are building HiveMind, a managed VA staffing company that places pre-vetted Filipino virtual assistants with U.S.-based small and medium businesses, with an initial focus on real estate. HiveMind competes with agencies like NexusPoint, Cyberbacker, and the fragmented mid-tier market.

The thesis in one line.

Better VA pay produces better retention, which produces better client outcomes and higher lifetime value, and a proprietary platform turns that talent into measurably superior, consistent service that incumbents running on spreadsheets cannot match.

What separates HiveMind from a typical new entrant is that the technology is not a future promise. The platform exists. We have built a seven-tool operations suite, a real-estate CRM with an embedded power dialer, and HiveMind AI, a desktop call copilot that transcribes VA-client calls in real time and coaches the VA live. This document is the technical companion to our investor deck: it sets out the market case briefly and the platform in depth.

02Market Opportunity

The Philippine advantage

The Philippines ranks #2 in Asia and #28 globally in English proficiency (EF EPI 2025, "High Proficiency"). With roughly 1.9 million IT-BPM workers plus a large freelance and VA sector, the talent pool is deep and well-educated, and cultural alignment with American business practices gives it an edge over India and Latin America for client-facing roles. North America accounts for about 70% of Philippine BPO client revenue, an established corridor of trust.

Target market: U.S. SMBs, starting with real estate

Our primary target is U.S. businesses with 1 to 50 employees, led by real estate agents and brokerages, then e-commerce operators, digital marketing agencies, and professional-service firms. These businesses need help but cannot justify $45,000 to $65,000 a year for a U.S. admin or marketing hire.

The value proposition in one sentence.

A full-time admin VA in the Philippines costs roughly $13,500 to $20,800 a year through agency placement, versus $45,000 to $65,000 for a U.S. equivalent.

Industry tailwinds

  • Remote-work normalization. U.S. businesses are now far more comfortable managing offshore staff.
  • AI augmentation. VAs who leverage AI are more productive, not displaced; HiveMind builds that augmentation into the product.
  • Specialization. Around 40% of VAs now offer specialized services rather than generic admin.
  • Policy support. IBPAP and the Philippine government target 2.5M IT-BPM workers by 2028.

04Business Model & Revenue Architecture

HiveMind runs a monthly subscription staffing model: clients pay a fixed monthly rate per VA, and we retain the margin between the client price and the fully-loaded VA cost. This is the dominant industry model, with typical gross margins between 25% and 50%.

The fair-pay advantage.

VAs earn $600 to $900 a month versus a ~$450 to $540 market average. Above-market pay converts directly into lower recruitment cost and lower client-facing churn, the metric clients actually feel.

Additional revenue streams

  • Setup / onboarding fee. One-time $250 to $500 per placement, covering vetting, skills testing, and client matching.
  • Platform license (Phase 2). The platform is purpose-built and can be licensed to other agencies, a second, higher-margin revenue line.
  • Training & upskilling. Premium certifications (AI tools, industry workflows) clients can request.

Why this stack matters.

Supabase Realtime is the backbone of the operating model: presence, broadcast events, and live row updates let a U.S. rep and a Manila VA work the same lead at the same moment. Real-time coordination is not a feature, it is the architecture.

5.3 · HiveMind AI: the desktop call copilot

HiveMind AI is a native desktop application that sits in on VA-client calls and makes a Filipino VA sound like a senior U.S. agent. It is the clearest demonstration that this is an engineering company, not a staffing shop with a website.

What it does live

  • Real-time transcription of both sides (mic + system audio), speaker-aware, ~300ms latency, on Zoom, Meet, and Teams
  • AI Coach: when a client asks something hard, it generates three answers, Safe, Sharp, and Bold, in one click
  • Live action items, decisions, and red flags, attributed VA vs. client
  • Auto meeting summary at call end, pushed into HiveFlow tasks

How it is built

  • Electron desktop shell with a low-footprint coaching overlay
  • Deepgram streaming speech-to-text
  • Three Claude agents: coach, summary, and action-items, each tuned to a narrow job
  • Synced to Supabase so the CRM and task board update as the call happens

5.4 · Real-time coordination & the power dialer

HiveMind's operating model has U.S. reps placing a small share of calls while Philippine operations handle the bulk, with both sides coordinating in real time. The platform makes that possible.

  • Embedded softphone. HiveCRM includes a SIP.js-based power dialer with click-to-call, call tones, and one-click dispositions, validated end-to-end.
  • Event bus. Call lifecycle events (call start, call end, call dispositioned) broadcast over Supabase Realtime, so the CRM auto-opens the active lead and logs outcomes without manual entry.
  • Presence. Reps and VAs see who is on a call and on which lead, the coordination layer incumbents simply do not have.

06Operations & Service Delivery

Client lifecycle

Every client runs the same disciplined path: Discovery (needs assessment and role scoping), Matching (a curated top-3 VA shortlist), Onboarding (a 7-day ramp with a tailored playbook), Managed support (PM check-ins, daily EOD reports, quarterly reviews), and Scale (add VAs or upgrade tiers).

VA recruitment funnel

We maintain a standing talent pool through job boards (OnlineJobs.ph, Kalibrr), social recruiting, university networks, and referrals. Vetting mirrors industry best practice with an added focus on AI-tool proficiency:

  1. Application screening: English, equipment, internet-speed verification
  2. Skills assessment: role-specific testing
  3. Paid work trial: a 2-hour simulation matching real client workflows
  4. Culture & communication interview: soft skills and fit
  5. Onboarding & training: 5 days on our platform, protocols, and AI tools
5-point EOD discipline, every day.

Accomplishments, in-progress, tomorrow's plan, blockers, and hours logged, generated in HiveReport and auto-delivered to the client, PM, and training team.

08Go-to-Market Strategy

Phase 1 · Foundation (Mo. 1–3)

  • Register PH entity + U.S. LLC for credibility
  • Harden platform for first cohort
  • Pre-vet 20 to 30 candidates
  • Land first 3 to 5 clients via referral and outreach

Phase 2 · Growth (Mo. 4–12)

  • Content + LinkedIn thought leadership
  • Paid acquisition to U.S. realtors and solopreneurs
  • Referral program ($200 credit per client)
  • Scale to 15 active placements

Phase 3 · Scale (Year 2+)

  • Vertical specialization (real estate, e-commerce, healthcare admin)
  • License the platform to other agencies (SaaS revenue)
  • Add multi-timezone talent markets (Colombia, DR)
  • Enterprise case studies and social proof
The U.S.-based partner is the single biggest accelerant.

Local presence builds the trust that purely offshore agencies struggle to establish, and unlocks the client relationships that drive recurring revenue. It is the one function the founding team does not already cover.

11Conclusion & Call to Action

The Philippine VA staffing industry is a $40B+ ecosystem growing at double digits, yet it remains operationally fragmented and runs on spreadsheets and chat apps. There is a clear opening for a tech-enabled, talent-first agency that treats its VAs well, owns its infrastructure, and delivers measurably better outcomes.

HiveMind brings four strengths most new entrants lack: in-house engineering with a platform already built, operator insight from inside competitor operations, design, and marketing, all covered by founders at a lean cost base.

What we need.

A U.S.-based partner who can be the face of the company in the American market: someone who can open doors, close deals, and build the client relationships that drive recurring revenue. In return we offer a co-founding role in a business with strong unit economics, a low startup cost, a large addressable market, and a real product that already works.
